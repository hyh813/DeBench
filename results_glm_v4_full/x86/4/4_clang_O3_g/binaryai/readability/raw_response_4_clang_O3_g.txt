**Overall Readability Score:** 5.8/10

**Critical Defects:**

**Best Quality Aspect:** Function Naming (Score: 8/10)
- **Why?** The decompiler successfully preserved the original symbol names for almost all test functions, making the high-level purpose of the code immediately identifiable.

**Most Needs Improvement:** Composite Type Recovery (Score: 2/10)
- **Why?** The complete failure to recover `LargeStruct` and `SmallStruct`, resulting in exploded parameter lists and generic pointers, is the single biggest barrier to understanding the code's data flow.

**Top 3 Recommendations for Decompiler Improvement:**
1. **Aggregate Parameter Recovery**: Implement logic to detect sequences of stack-passed parameters that correspond to struct-by-value passing and reconstruct the composite type definition.
2. **64-bit Integer Reconstruction**: On 32-bit architectures, automatically detect pairs of registers/stack slots used for 64-bit integers (`long long`) and merge them into a single `long long` or `int64` variable.
3. **Varargs Idiom Replacement**: Recognize the standard stack-walking patterns used for `va_list` and replace raw pointer arithmetic with pseudo-code or comments indicating `va_arg` usage.
