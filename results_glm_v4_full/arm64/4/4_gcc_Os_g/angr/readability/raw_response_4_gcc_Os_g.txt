**Overall Readability Score:** 5.2/10

**Most Needs Improvement:** Composite Type Recovery (Score: 2/10)  
- The failure to recover structs (both small and large) forces the reader to understand ABI details (register packing, hidden pointers) manually, which is a major barrier to understanding.

**Top 3 Recommendations for Decompiler Improvement:**
1.  **Implement Struct Return/Pass Recovery**: Detect when multiple registers or stack slots combine to form a struct (especially for small structs returned in registers) and reconstruct the composite type definition.
2.  **Recognize Varargs Patterns**: Identify the standard `va_list` prologue/epilogue patterns and abstract the complex stack pointer arithmetic into standard `va_start`, `va_arg`, etc.
3.  **Improve Floating-Point Type Inference**: Better distinguish between floating-point and general-purpose registers in the signature analysis to prevent `double` parameters from being lost or misinterpreted as integers.
