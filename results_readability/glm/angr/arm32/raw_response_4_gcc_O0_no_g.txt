### === FINAL SUMMARY ===

**Overall Readability Score:** 3.7/10

**Critical Defects:**
- **Struct Loss**: `LargeStruct` and `SmallStruct` are completely lost, replaced by raw pointer arithmetic and array indexing (e.g., `func_struct_byval`).

**Best Quality Aspect:** Function Naming (scored 8/10)

**Most Needs Improvement:** Composite Type Recovery (scored 1/10)
- The complete inability to recover structures (either passed by value or returned) forces the reader to interpret raw memory offsets, which is the single biggest barrier to understanding the data flow.

**Top 3 Recommendations for Decompiler Improvement:**
1. **Implement Struct Recovery**: Analyze stack layouts and register usage to reconstruct composite types (structs/unions) passed by value or returned, replacing pointer arithmetic with field access.
2. **Filter Library Code**: Detect and exclude or abstract standard library/compiler-rt functions (like soft-float `__aeabi` routines) to prevent them from polluting the decompilation output.
3. **Recognize Varargs Patterns**: Identify standard `va_list` implementation patterns (stack pointer walking) and re-abstract them into `va_start`/`va_arg` idioms to improve semantic transparency.
